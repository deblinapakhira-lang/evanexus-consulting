About

I'm a convening strategist
and researcher.

My work sits upstream of logistics — deciding who belongs in the room, what questions move the work forward, and how conversation turns into decisions.

Deblina Pakhira, PhD · San Francisco Bay Area

I help foundations, research organizations, health and education institutions design and facilitate high-stakes convenings that generate real decisions — not just good conversations. I bring a researcher's rigor and a practitioner's instinct to every engagement: every session starts with a clear question, uses the right method to surface what matters, and produces insight that is actually used.

I've built cross-sector partnerships spanning school districts, higher education institutions, employers, community organizations, foundations, and government agencies, consistently centering access for historically underserved communities.

That rigor comes from a research background: a Ph.D. in Chemistry & Chemistry Education, peer-reviewed and public research on equity-centered research design, and national presentations including the ASU GSV Summit. My convenings have shaped a national research funder's strategy, informed policy recommendations now under review by California's incoming administration, and built the intellectual architecture behind conferences reaching hundreds of researchers and practitioners nationally.

If you need someone to decide who belongs in the room and how their answers become a plan you can act on — that's the work I do.
