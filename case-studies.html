CIRCLS'23 National Convening

Shaping AI and Emerging Technologies to Empower Learning Communities

Nov 2–3, 2023 · NSF-funded · Digital Promise, U. Pittsburgh, EDC, SRI Education

The Challenge

The national RETTL research community needed a shared space to move from reactive technology proliferation toward a considerate, equity-grounded design process for AI in education — and to leave with a forward-looking research agenda.

The Approach

A two-day architecture layering roundtables, structured "expertise connections," a gallery walk, and keynotes — sequenced deliberately to build toward a closing town hall with NSF program officers.

The Execution

Managed end-to-end: coordinating across NSF and four partner institutions, recruiting a national multidisciplinary base, and directing on-site production and real-time facilitation.

The Outcome

Insights directly informed CIRCLS's 2023 Community Report, Partnerships for Change, strengthening the feedback loop between the research community and its funder.

Regions Up Convenings

Building a Durable, Regions-Up Economic Model for California

Spring 2026 · California Forward & California Stewardship Network

The Challenge

With $1.4B in regional investments expiring and a new governor arriving in 2027, CA FWD needed grounded, statewide input — without letting any single region's voice dominate the findings.

The Approach

Three regional convenings in Fresno, Long Beach, and Redding, deliberately spanning central, southern, and northern California, with a genuinely cross-sector stakeholder set in every room.

The Execution

Co-led the three-convening series — coordinating logistics and stakeholder engagement, and facilitating cross-sector dialogue across widely varying regional priorities.

The Outcome

Five cross-regional themes and a concrete slate of solutions, now directly informing a policy playbook for California's incoming administration and legislature.

SEERNet Office Hours

A Structured Strategy for Researcher-Practitioner Research Question Co-Design

2023 · IES-funded · Digital Promise & Empirical Education

The Challenge

SEERNet needed a concrete, repeatable mechanism for bringing practitioners into the research process early enough to shape the research questions themselves, across five very different Digital Learning Platforms.

The Approach

I led Office Hours end to end — a structured co-design strategy run over three months, with a deliberate arc of guiding questions, live Q&A, synthesis, and participant feedback for each DLP.

The Execution

31 participants across five DLP sessions produced five DLP-specific research question documents, each refined through live synthesis and a feedback round.

The Outcome

That initial convening grew into a sustained practitioner advisory board and a researcher network — ultimately becoming the foundation for Digital Promise's Research-Practice-Industry Partnership framework.
