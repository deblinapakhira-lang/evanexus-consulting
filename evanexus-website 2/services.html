Services

Three tiers of
engagement.

The right level of involvement depends on how much of the thinking — not just the doing — you need me to hold. Each tier below reflects a real difference in depth, not just scope.

01

Convening Design Sprint

Strategy + Architecture

For organizations that have a convening coming up and need expert design support — but have their own facilitation team in place.

  • Research-informed participant strategy & invitation design
  • Agenda architecture built around a clear central question
  • Discussion guides grounded in evidence, not just talking points
  • Facilitator preparation & coaching to ensure rigor on the day
  • Post-convening synthesis framework so insights get used, not shelved
02

Full Convening Partnership

Design + Facilitation + Synthesis

End-to-end ownership of a single convening or series — from participant strategy through final synthesis report and action tracking.

  • Participant & stakeholder mapping grounded in who needs to be in the room
  • Full agenda design translating strategic goals into working sessions
  • Day-of facilitation that surfaces real tensions, not surface-level consensus
  • Synthesis & findings report turning conversation into a decision-ready document
  • Action item tracking to ensure recommendations translate into follow-through
03

Convening Program Design

Multi-Convening Series & Learning Ecosystem

For organizations running ongoing working groups, research networks, or communities of practice that need sustained convening design across a program year.

  • Multi-convening strategy & annual research and engagement arc design
  • Working group structure grounded in co-design, not one-way consultation
  • Knowledge-sharing infrastructure that compounds insight between sessions
  • Ongoing facilitation & relationship management across the network
  • Program-level synthesis & impact reporting for funders and leadership

How Engagements Work

Every tier follows the same underlying discipline.

Before the room

I start with a clear question, not a run-of-show. Who needs to be in the room, and what does this convening need to produce for it to be worth everyone's time? Participant strategy and agenda architecture flow from that answer.

In the room

Facilitation is built to surface real tension and disagreement, not manufacture polite consensus. The right method for the right question — roundtables, working sessions, structured co-design, town halls.

After the room

A convening that ends when the room empties hasn't done its job. Every engagement includes a synthesis process that turns conversation into a decision-ready document.

Sustained impact

For ongoing networks and working groups, the relationships and knowledge-sharing infrastructure between sessions matter as much as any single convening.

Not sure which tier fits?

Most engagements start with a short conversation about what you're trying to accomplish — and what "done well" actually looks like for your organization.
